# Pilot Metrics

**Status: proposed / draft.**

This document defines what success looks like for the Noosphere City Gazipaşa pilot — and what counts as a failed validation. Metrics are public by design. No second cohort is launched without an honest accounting of whether the first cohort met them.

---

## Leading indicators (pre-pilot)

These are tracked before the pilot launches to determine whether it is safe to proceed.

- Number of founders / builders who expressed serious interest
- Number of local partners with confirmed or near-confirmed agreements
- Number of real challenge owners (not nominal ones)
- Number of housing or venue leads at different commitment levels
- Number of discovery calls completed
- Number of signed or exchanged non-binding partner letters
- Number of prototype commitments from accepted founders

**Gate:** The pilot should not launch without at least the lean pilot minimum threshold met (see below).

---

## Pilot metrics (during and at completion)

These are measured during and at the end of the pilot.

### Participation
- Residents selected (target: 3–10 depending on format)
- Residents who completed the full program
- Challenge tracks active at day 30, day 60, and day 90
- Local stakeholders engaged (partners, business owners, users)

### Output
- Prototypes started
- Prototypes that reached demo quality by demo day
- Local user tests completed (with real users, not internal only)
- Workshops delivered to local community
- Demo-day attendance (partners, residents, press, public)

### Economic
- Total cost of pilot (operator time + housing + workspace + logistics)
- Cost per completed prototype
- Cost per resident
- Sponsors or challenge owners who contributed in-kind or financially
- Continuation opportunities identified (paid work, second cohort interest, partnerships)

### Satisfaction
- Resident satisfaction score (simple 1–5 survey, published in report)
- Local partner satisfaction score (simple 1–5 survey, published in report)
- Challenge owner satisfaction (did the prototype address their problem?)

---

## Trust metrics

These measure whether the pilot was conducted with integrity.

- Number of public artifacts (repositories, write-ups, demos)
- Final public report published (yes / no)
- Failures and non-completions disclosed in the report (yes / no)
- Legal review completed before launch (yes / no)
- Partner agreements documented in writing before cohort start (yes / no)
- Data handling reviewed under KVKK / GDPR before any data was collected (yes / no)

---

## Minimum validation thresholds

### Lean pilot

The lean pilot is considered validated if ALL of the following are met:

- [ ] 1 confirmed anchor partner (housing or venue)
- [ ] 3–5 founders/builders selected and completed the program
- [ ] 2 local challenge owners actively participated
- [ ] 2 working prototypes demonstrated at demo day
- [ ] 1 public report published (honest, including failures)

### Standard pilot

The standard pilot is considered validated if ALL of the following are met:

- [ ] 2–3 local partners confirmed with written agreements
- [ ] 5–10 founders/builders selected and completed the program
- [ ] 3–5 challenge tracks with named, active challenge owners
- [ ] 3–5 working prototypes demonstrated at demo day
- [ ] 1 public demo day event attended by partners, press, and local community
- [ ] 1 paid or partner-supported continuation path identified
- [ ] 1 public report published (honest, including failures)

---

## Failure conditions

The pilot is considered not validated if any of the following are true:

- No anchor partner confirmed before launch
- Fewer than 3 credible founders/builders committed by start date
- No real local challenge owner participated
- No prototype reached demo quality
- No partner expressed interest in continuation after the pilot
- Legal or operator pathway remained unresolved at launch
- Public report was not published within 30 days of pilot end

If validation fails, see the failure scenario in [docs/pilot_90_day_plan.md](../docs/pilot_90_day_plan.md).

---

## How metrics will be published

All metrics will be included in the public 90-day report. The report will be published in this repository and linked from the landing page. Failure and underperformance will be disclosed, not hidden.
