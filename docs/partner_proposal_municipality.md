# Partner Proposal — Gazipaşa Municipality

**Status: proposed / draft. Not yet shared with the municipality.**

This document is a one-page-style proposal for the Gazipaşa Municipality. The intent is to start a conversation, not to ask for funding or apartments.

## Summary

We propose a 90-day pilot of an AI founder residency in Gazipaşa. A small group of selected builders would work on practical tracks tied to local needs — tourism, agriculture, real estate transparency, smart city services, environmental monitoring, and education. Housing would be arranged with private partners. The municipality would be invited as a civic partner, not a funder.

## What we ask from the municipality

- A non-binding letter of support or memorandum of understanding for the pilot.
- Help in framing 1–3 concrete city challenges that the residency can work on.
- A meeting venue for the demo day.
- Introductions to relevant local stakeholders.
- Permission to publish a public report at the end of the pilot.

## What we do **not** ask

- No municipal funding.
- No apartments from public stock.
- No exclusive commitments.
- No regulatory exceptions.

## What the municipality receives

- 3–5 working prototypes targeting Gazipaşa-specific problems.
- A public 90-day report documenting the pilot and its outcomes.
- At least one free public workshop for residents or children.
- International visibility for Gazipaşa as a forward-looking, tech-friendly district.
- A clear basis to decide whether to continue, expand, or close the program after 90 days.

## Risk profile for the municipality

- **Financial risk:** none in the pilot (no public funds requested).
- **Reputational risk:** controlled. The pilot is framed as a draft initiative. The municipality is a civic partner, not the owner.
- **Operational risk:** low. The residency runs its own selection, housing, and programming.

## Proposed sequence

1. Initial meeting to align on scope and language.
2. Letter of support or simple MoU.
3. Joint definition of 1–3 city challenges.
4. Pilot launch with selected residents.
5. Demo day and public report.
6. Joint review: continue, adjust, or close.

## What success looks like for the municipality

- Tangible improvements or prototypes residents can see and use.
- Positive coverage of Gazipaşa as a place where modern technology serves the city.
- A clear, documented decision point at day 90.
