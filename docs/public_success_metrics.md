# Public Success Metrics

**Status: draft targets.** These are not promises. They are pilot validation targets that allow the project, partners, and the public to judge whether the first pilot actually worked.

## Lean pilot target (30–45 days)

- 3–5 founders / builders in residence
- 1 anchor partner
- 2–3 local challenge tracks
- 2 working prototypes
- 1 public demo day
- 1 public report
- 1 possible continuation path identified

## Standard pilot target (60–90 days)

- 5–10 founders / builders in residence
- 2–3 anchor or local partners
- 3–5 local challenge tracks
- 3–5 working prototypes
- 1 public demo day
- 1 public report
- at least 1 paid or partner-supported continuation path

## Honesty rule

If the targets are not met, the public report must say so honestly. The report should include:

- which targets were met and which were missed,
- why,
- what would change in a second pilot,
- whether a second pilot is worth running.

A pilot that is reported honestly — even if it underperforms — is more valuable to future partners than a pilot that is reported optimistically.

## What we do not promise

- We do not promise specific funding amounts.
- We do not promise visas, work permits, or residency status.
- We do not promise that prototypes will become companies.
- We do not promise that partners will receive financial returns.
- We do not promise municipal endorsement.
