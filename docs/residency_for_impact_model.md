# Residency-for-Impact Model

**Status: proposed / draft.**

The Noosphere City residency is built on a simple exchange: founders receive housing, workspace, and access; the city receives working pilots, documentation, and public events. Nothing is given without something in return.

## Core principles

1. **Exchange, not charity.** Housing is not a gift. It is part of a documented agreement.
2. **Local impact first.** Every resident commits to one concrete deliverable that benefits Gazipaşa.
3. **Open by default.** Documentation, write-ups, and (where possible) code are public.
4. **Short and intense.** Residencies are 1–3 months. Long enough to ship, short enough to stay focused.
5. **Respect the place.** Founders are guests. Local norms, language, and pace come first.

## What residents receive

- Temporary housing (1–3 months).
- Access to a shared workspace and basic infrastructure.
- A small, curated founder community.
- Introductions to relevant local stakeholders.
- A slot on the demo day.
- A public mention in the cohort report.

## What residents commit to

- One civic or business pilot tied to a chosen track.
- Weekly progress notes shared with the cohort.
- At least one public-facing artifact: a workshop, a talk, a write-up, an open repository, or a demo.
- Participation in the final demo day.
- A short post-residency reflection for the public report.

## Resident profile

- AI, software, or hardware builders.
- Willing to ship something concrete in 90 days.
- Comfortable working in English.
- Open to a small-town Mediterranean environment.
- Respectful of local culture and stakeholders.

## Selection criteria

- Relevance to one of the published challenge tracks.
- Demonstrated ability to ship (prior projects, GitHub history, prototypes).
- Realistic 90-day plan.
- Willingness to share artifacts publicly.
- Diversity of backgrounds and disciplines across the cohort.

## What disqualifies an applicant

- Pure tourism intent.
- Unwillingness to engage with local stakeholders.
- Projects that extract value from Gazipaşa without giving back.
- Vague or inflated claims with no track record.

## Failure modes to avoid

- A residency that becomes a cheap co-living retreat with no civic output.
- Founders treating the city as a backdrop rather than a partner.
- Local partners feeling used after the cohort leaves.
- Over-promising specific government support that has not been secured.

## Lifecycle of a resident

1. **Apply.** Short written application + portfolio.
2. **Match.** Resident is matched to a challenge track and, where possible, a local stakeholder.
3. **Arrive.** Onboarding session, housing handover, code of conduct, local context briefing.
4. **Build.** Weekly check-ins, biweekly community sessions.
5. **Ship.** Demo day presentation and public artifact.
6. **Reflect.** Short written reflection for the public report.
7. **Stay connected.** Optional alumni network for future cohorts and partners.
