# Resident Value Proposition

**Status: proposed / draft.**

This document answers a simple question: why would a founder, researcher, or builder come to Gazipaşa instead of another hub?

## Why would a founder come to Gazipaşa?

Founders do not come only for free housing. They come if the residency gives them:

- lower burn rate,
- focus,
- real-world pilot access,
- local partner access,
- public proof of work,
- community,
- and a route to commercial or open-source validation.

## Resident benefits

- reduced living costs,
- temporary housing or housing support,
- real local challenge access,
- public proof of work,
- demo day,
- partner introductions,
- local pilot opportunities,
- GitHub and other public portfolio artifacts,
- focused environment near nature and the sea,
- access to Turkish startup ecosystem pathways,
- possible technopark or incubator introductions,
- practical feedback from local businesses.

## Resident obligations

- build a useful prototype,
- document work publicly where appropriate,
- respect local laws and community,
- participate in weekly reviews,
- contribute to final demo day,
- avoid misleading claims,
- leave reusable knowledge after the pilot,
- follow participant terms and local partner rules.

## Commercialization paths

Possible paths after the pilot:

- local business pilot,
- SaaS product,
- open-source support model,
- grant application,
- technopark or incubator application,
- partner-funded continuation,
- investor demo,
- municipal or regional pilot,
- tourism, agriculture, or education product.

## Why Gazipaşa instead of a major hub?

- lower cost than major startup hubs,
- calmer environment,
- access to real local needs,
- tourism, agriculture, and environment challenges,
- proximity to Mediterranean lifestyle,
- potential airport and regional access,
- ability to test a small-city innovation model.

Gazipaşa may offer these advantages, but the pilot must validate whether they are strong enough to attract and retain founders.

## Retention risk

A key risk is that founders may leave after the pilot. Mitigation:

- create commercial pilot pathways,
- offer follow-up partner introductions,
- create an alumni network,
- help with technopark and incubator pathways,
- produce public artifacts that increase founder career value.
