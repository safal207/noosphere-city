# First Partner Outreach Plan

**Status: draft.** A 30-day plan to move from "documents on GitHub" to "first real anchor partner conversation."

## Goals

By the end of 30 days, the project should either:

- have one anchor partner candidate willing to discuss a written, non-binding pilot outline, **or**
- have a clear, evidence-based decision to pause, redesign, or change location.

## Week 1 — Shortlist and first contact

- Build a shortlist of 10–15 candidate organizations across:
  - apart-hotels and hotels in Gazipaşa with low-season capacity,
  - developers and real estate agencies,
  - local businesses and tourism operators,
  - farms, greenhouses, cooperatives,
  - schools and education partners.
- Score each candidate using [anchor_partner_scorecard.md](anchor_partner_scorecard.md).
- Prepare the one-page partner brief ([../templates/one_page_partner_brief.md](../templates/one_page_partner_brief.md)).
- Send 5 warm or soft outreach messages using the templates in `templates/`.

## Week 2 — Discovery calls

- Run 3–5 discovery calls (15–30 minutes each).
- In each call, ask:
  - Do you have unused or low-season capacity?
  - What local problems do you actually care about?
  - Would you be open to a 30–45 day lean pilot?
  - What would make this not work for you?
- Do not pitch. Listen and record what they say.
- Update scorecards after each call.

## Week 3 — Narrow to candidates

- Select 1–2 serious candidates.
- Draft a non-binding pilot outline tailored to each.
- Identify the legal questions a real pilot would need answered (housing terms, hosting status, public communication, liability).
- Share the outline only after the partner asks for a next step.

## Week 4 — Decide

- Choose one anchor partner candidate, if one is willing.
- Define one local challenge to prototype around.
- Decide one of:
  - **continue** — move toward a written, non-binding pilot outline and begin local legal review,
  - **pause** — keep the project in discovery and adjust the approach,
  - **redesign** — change location, partner type, or pilot shape.

## Tracking

For each candidate, track:

- date of first contact,
- channel,
- scorecard total,
- response status,
- key concerns raised,
- next step.

A simple table in a private document is enough. Do not publish partner names or scores.
