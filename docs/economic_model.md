# Economic Model

**Status: proposed / draft.**

This document explains the economic logic of Noosphere City Gazipaşa as a pilot, not as a mature business. It is intended to make the proposal credible for partners while avoiding promises that cannot yet be supported.

## Current status

- Noosphere City Gazipaşa is a draft pilot proposal.
- It has no confirmed municipal approval.
- It has no confirmed housing partners.
- It has no confirmed investors.
- It has no operating entity yet.
- It has no revenue yet.

The first pilot should validate the economic model before any scaling decision is made.

## Core exchange

Local partners may provide:

- temporary housing or housing discounts,
- workspace or meeting space,
- access to real local challenges,
- local introductions,
- visibility,
- operational support.

Selected founders and builders may provide:

- useful prototypes,
- workshops,
- documentation,
- public reports,
- GitHub artifacts,
- local pilots,
- demo-day presentations,
- practical solutions for tourism, agriculture, education, environment, real estate transparency, local business, and smart city services.

Noosphere City is not based on "free apartments for startups." It is based on a residency-for-impact exchange: local support in return for practical, documented, and reviewable local innovation work.

## Resident economic incentives

Residents may benefit from:

- reduced cost of living compared with major tech hubs,
- temporary subsidized or partner-supported housing,
- a focused build environment,
- access to real-world pilot opportunities,
- public proof of work,
- demo day exposure,
- local business introductions,
- connection to Turkish startup ecosystem pathways,
- possible commercial pilots after the residency,
- portfolio artifacts and public credibility,
- a quieter environment near nature and the sea.

Founders come not only for housing support, but for a lower-burn environment where they can build, test, document, and validate useful products with real local stakeholders.

## Municipal and regional value

The municipality may benefit from:

- low-cost innovation discovery,
- tourism and investment branding,
- practical prototypes for local challenges,
- international visibility,
- stronger link to AI and startup ecosystems,
- workshops for local youth and businesses,
- public reports showing what was learned,
- a controlled experiment in talent attraction.

These are possible and to be validated. They are not guaranteed tax revenue, jobs, or investment.

## Municipal multiplier hypothesis

A small 90-day pilot could test whether:

- 5–10 residents can produce 3–5 useful prototypes,
- 1–2 prototypes can become local pilots,
- 1–3 local businesses can become active challenge partners,
- at least one partner wants to continue after the pilot,
- public visibility improves Gazipaşa's innovation image.

These are pilot hypotheses, not promises.

## Economic flywheel

1. Housing or local partners contribute unused or low-season capacity.
2. Founders reduce living costs and build prototypes.
3. Local businesses and institutions provide real problems.
4. Demo day creates visibility and trust.
5. Useful prototypes become pilots.
6. Pilots create case studies.
7. Case studies attract more partners, founders, sponsors, and funding.

## Revenue sources to test

Possible revenue sources, none of them confirmed:

- partner sponsorship,
- paid residency seats after the pilot,
- corporate challenge fees,
- demo day sponsorship,
- workshops and training,
- local business subscriptions for selected tools,
- grant funding as acceleration capital,
- technopark or incubator support,
- philanthropic or civic innovation support,
- success fees only if legally reviewed.

No revenue source is confirmed yet. The pilot is meant to test which of these can realistically produce meaningful income.

## Token / credits note

Any token, credit, contribution-point, or reputation mechanism is **not part of the first pilot's financial model**.

It may be explored later only as:

- a non-speculative contribution accounting layer,
- an internal recognition mechanism,
- or a governance and reputation experiment,

and only after legal, tax, and regulatory review.

The first pilot should use conventional budgeting, written partner agreements, transparent reporting, and simple accounting. It must not include a token launch, an investment token, a speculative token, a promise of token value, or resident compensation via token.

## Sustainability risks

- Founders leave after the pilot.
- No partner pays after the first cohort.
- Prototypes do not become pilots.
- Housing partners withdraw.
- Municipal reputation risk.
- Legal or visa uncertainty.
- Weak resident incentives.
- No anchor partner.
- Dependence on grants only.

## Mitigation

- Start small.
- Use clear participant selection.
- Require written partner agreements.
- Define challenge owners.
- Use weekly reviews.
- Publish a final report.
- Avoid unsupported promises.
- Focus on practical local pilots.
- Keep tokens out of the first pilot.
- Validate at least one paid continuation path.
