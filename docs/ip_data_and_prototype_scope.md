# IP, Data, and Prototype Scope

**Status: proposed / draft.**

This document clarifies the intellectual property, data, and prototype responsibility boundaries for the Noosphere City Gazipaşa pilot. It is not a legal agreement. Before any pilot launches, these matters must be addressed in written agreements reviewed by qualified counsel.

---

## Prototype status

Outputs created by founders during the pilot are prototypes — research-and-development artifacts — unless a separate written agreement explicitly defines a different status.

Prototypes should not be treated as production-ready systems. They are not:
- Approved for deployment in live operational environments
- Guaranteed to be secure, bug-free, or reliable at scale
- Covered by any warranty, SLA, or support obligation
- Reviewed for compliance with sector-specific regulations (medical, financial, critical infrastructure, etc.)

A prototype may be used for testing, demonstration, and proof-of-concept purposes only. Any further use requires a separate written review, approval, and responsibility assignment.

---

## IP ownership

**Default assumption:** Founders retain full ownership of intellectual property they create during the pilot, unless a separate written agreement between the founder and a partner defines otherwise.

This includes:
- Code, algorithms, models, and datasets created by the founder.
- Documentation, write-ups, and design work.
- Any other creative or technical output.

**Partner access:** Local partners may receive pilot and testing access to prototypes under clearly defined written terms. Such terms must specify:
- What the partner may do with the prototype during the pilot.
- What happens to the prototype after the pilot ends.
- Whether the partner may use, modify, or commercialize the prototype.
- Whether and how the founder is compensated if the prototype is commercialized.

**No implied transfer:** Providing housing, workspace, or challenge access does not imply any transfer of IP rights from founder to partner. Any IP transfer requires an explicit written agreement.

---

## Liability and bugs

No prototype developed during the pilot should be deployed in a live operational setting without:
1. Independent technical review and testing.
2. Clear written approval from the deploying party.
3. Explicit written assignment of legal and operational responsibility.
4. Appropriate insurance and regulatory clearance where applicable.

The Noosphere City operator and program framework do not assume liability for prototypes created by residents. Each founder is responsible for their own outputs. Each partner is responsible for their own decision to deploy or test any prototype.

---

## Data

### Personal data

Any collection, storage, or processing of personal data during the pilot requires:
- A clear legal basis under Turkish law (KVKK — Kişisel Verilerin Korunması Kanunu).
- Where EU residents or citizens are involved, compliance with GDPR.
- A privacy notice explaining what data is collected, how it is used, and how it can be deleted.
- Review and approval before any data collection begins.

Founders should not collect personal data from local residents, tourists, or business users without this framework in place.

### Partner and business data

Data provided by local businesses, farms, or municipal partners (e.g. operational data, financial data, location data) must not be:
- Published, open-sourced, or shared publicly without explicit written permission from the data owner.
- Used beyond the agreed scope of the challenge track.
- Retained after the pilot ends without a separate data-sharing or licensing agreement.

---

## Open-source outputs

If a founder chooses to open-source their prototype:
- The license must be explicitly chosen and stated (e.g. MIT, Apache 2.0, GPL, CC BY).
- The founder must confirm they have the right to open-source all components, including any third-party libraries or data.
- Any partner or challenge-owner data must be removed or anonymized before open-sourcing.

The pilot encourages open-source outputs but does not require them.

---

## Contributions from multiple parties

If a prototype involves contributions from multiple founders, or from a founder and a local partner or challenge owner, the ownership and licensing terms must be agreed in writing before significant contributions are made.
