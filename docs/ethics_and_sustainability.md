# Ethics and Sustainability

**Status: proposed / draft.**

A residency that brings outsiders into a small district has obligations. This document records the principles Noosphere City Gazipaşa intends to operate by, and the failure modes it intends to avoid.

## Principles

1. **Be a guest first.** Residents are visitors in someone else's home. Local norms, language, and pace come first.
2. **Honest framing.** No claims of approvals, partners, or outcomes that have not been confirmed. The word "proposed" is not a weakness; it is honesty.
3. **Open by default.** Documentation, write-ups, and code are public unless there is a specific reason otherwise.
4. **Local benefit before extraction.** No track ships if it only extracts value from Gazipaşa.
5. **Respect for nature.** Coastal, agricultural, and protected areas are treated as long-term assets, not props.
6. **Clear consent.** Anyone whose data, image, or work is used must give informed consent.
7. **Reasonable scale.** Small cohorts. Small footprint. Slow growth.

## Specific commitments

- No glossy marketing of Gazipaşa as a "tech hub" until there is something real to show.
- No pressure on the municipality or partners to sign anything they have not had time to read.
- No use of children's images or names in promotional material without explicit guardian consent.
- No collection of personal data from residents or partners beyond what is needed for the pilot.
- All public artifacts are reviewed for accuracy before publication.

## Sustainability dimensions

### Environmental
- Prefer existing buildings over new construction.
- Use low-season housing capacity instead of pushing peak demand.
- Encourage residents to walk, cycle, and use shared transport.
- Treat Caretta Caretta nesting areas, beaches, and water resources as protected by default.

### Social
- Free workshops for residents and children every cohort.
- Local liaison roles paid fairly.
- Cohort selection that does not concentrate one nationality, gender, or background.
- A clear, written code of conduct with a path to report issues.

### Economic
- Spend as much as possible with local businesses: groceries, food, services.
- Do not displace long-term residents in the housing market. Use vacant or seasonal stock only.
- No speculative real estate activity by the residency itself.

### Governance
- A small advisory group that includes at least one local resident and one external reviewer.
- An annual public report, even if a cohort is skipped.
- A documented procedure for ending the program cleanly if it stops being beneficial.

## Failure modes we explicitly want to avoid

- A "digital nomad" rebrand with no civic output.
- Real estate marketing dressed as a residency.
- Tech-saviorism that ignores local knowledge.
- Burnout of a single founder or organizer holding everything together.
- Quiet drift away from the original civic purpose.

## Participant and partner protection

The residency only works if everyone involved is treated fairly. The following protections apply by default.

- **No misleading promises to founders.** No guarantees of visas, employment, investment, free long-term housing, or specific outcomes. What is offered is described in writing; what is not offered is not implied.
- **No pressure on local partners.** Municipalities, developers, hotels, agencies, schools, and farmers are free to decline, pause, or withdraw at any stage without penalty or public criticism.
- **No extraction of free labor from participants.** Residents are not asked to deliver unpaid commercial work for partners outside the agreed civic or pilot scope. Work done for a partner beyond that scope is governed by a separate written agreement.
- **No displacement or housing pressure on local residents.** Only vacant, seasonal, or otherwise underused stock is used for housing. The residency does not compete with long-term local renters or buyers.
- **Respect for local language, culture, ecology, and legal requirements.** Communication is offered in Turkish where possible. Local norms, religious and cultural practices, protected natural areas, and Turkish law are followed by default, not as an afterthought.

These protections are minimum standards. Specific cohorts may add more, but may not weaken them.

## Economic sustainability and fairness

- Do not use free founder labor without a clear value exchange.
- Do not promise housing, visas, investment, jobs, or customers unless formal agreements exist.
- Do not pressure local businesses into unpaid work.
- Do not create housing pressure for local residents.
- Do not use token or speculative mechanisms in the first pilot.
- Make resident obligations clear before acceptance.
- Publish honest outcomes after the pilot, including failures.

## How we will know we are off track

- Local partners stop returning calls.
- Residents do not produce public artifacts.
- Workshops are cancelled more often than they happen.
- The narrative starts to focus on the program's brand instead of Gazipaşa.

If two or more of these signals appear, the residency lead is expected to pause, write a short honest review, and either correct course or close the program.
