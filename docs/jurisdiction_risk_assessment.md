# Jurisdiction Risk Assessment

**Status: proposed / draft.**

Noosphere City cannot be launched safely in every country. The model depends heavily on jurisdiction quality. This document records the screening logic.

## Why jurisdiction matters

Noosphere City depends on:

- international founders,
- housing partners,
- local businesses,
- public communication,
- data,
- internet access,
- payments,
- visas and residence,
- trust,
- legal predictability,
- local operational partners.

A weak or high-risk jurisdiction can break the model even if the concept is strong.

## Minimum jurisdiction criteria

- predictable legal environment,
- ability to host international participants,
- reasonable visa or residence pathways,
- access to banking and payment infrastructure,
- stable internet and local infrastructure,
- low reputational risk for international founders,
- local partner willingness,
- municipal or ecosystem openness,
- manageable inflation and cost-planning risk,
- low sanctions and capital-control exposure,
- reasonable safety and quality-of-life conditions.

## High-risk jurisdiction indicators

- sanctions exposure,
- capital-control exposure,
- weak investor confidence,
- unstable municipal finances,
- high inflation or unreliable cost planning,
- international isolation,
- high reputational risk for foreign participants,
- unclear rules for NGOs, associations, startups, or foreign-funded activity,
- difficulty attracting global founders,
- weak rule-of-law perception,
- difficulty using international payments or cloud services.

## Russia-like stress-test scenario

This is a stress test, not an operational target.

A Russia-like high-risk macro and geopolitical environment illustrates why jurisdiction screening matters. Even a strong civic innovation concept may become impractical if international founders cannot safely participate, investors avoid the market, banking and payment channels are restricted, public collaboration becomes reputationally risky, or municipal partners face fiscal stress.

This framing is not legal advice and is not a political claim. The project does not propose to operate in Russia. The scenario is used only to test the screening criteria.

## Why Gazipaşa / Türkiye remains the proposed first pilot context

The current proposal is Türkiye-first and Gazipaşa-first because the pilot is intended to test:

- a lower-cost coastal founder environment,
- tourism, agriculture, and local business challenge tracks,
- international founder attraction,
- partner-supported housing,
- links to Turkish startup ecosystem pathways,
- a small-city innovation model.

Türkiye is not risk-free. Any real launch still requires legal, visa, tax, entity, housing, and partner review.

## Decision rule

If a jurisdiction creates unresolved visa, banking, reputational, sanctions, housing, municipal, or legal-operator risks, it should not host the first pilot.

## Recommended position

- **First pilot:** Gazipaşa / Türkiye, if partners and a legal pathway are validated.
- **Other jurisdictions:** evaluate only after applying the risk criteria above.
- **High-risk jurisdictions:** use as stress tests, not launch targets.
