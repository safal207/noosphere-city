# Anchor Partner Strategy

**Status: draft.** This document describes how Noosphere City Gazipaşa will look for its first anchor partner. It does not name any confirmed partner. No municipality, hotel, developer, or business has signed any agreement.

## Why an anchor partner matters

A pilot proposal is only a document until at least one real partner says: "We have a problem, a venue, or a room, and we are open to a small experiment." The first anchor partner converts the project from an idea on GitHub into a real conversation grounded in a specific place, a specific room, or a specific local problem.

The first anchor partner does not need to fund the whole project. They only need to help validate one part of the pilot.

## What an anchor partner can provide

An anchor partner can offer one or more of the following:

- low-season housing capacity (apart-hotel rooms, unused apartments),
- a venue for workshops, demo days, or co-working,
- access to a real local challenge (tourism, agriculture, education, retail, environment),
- introductions to other local stakeholders,
- public visibility and credibility in Gazipaşa,
- review of communication so claims stay accurate.

## Types of anchor partners

1. **Apart-hotel or hotel** with low-season capacity.
2. **Real estate developer** with unsold or unused inventory.
3. **Real estate agency** with relationships across Gazipaşa.
4. **Local business or tourism operator** with a real, owned problem.
5. **Farm, greenhouse, or agricultural cooperative.**
6. **School or education partner** open to AI workshops.
7. **Municipality department** that can introduce stakeholders.
8. **Technopark or incubator** outside Gazipaşa willing to umbrella the pilot.

## Best first partner type

For a first pilot, the most realistic anchor is an **apart-hotel or hotel with low-season capacity**, or a **developer / real estate agency with unused inventory**. Both have:

- a clear underused asset (rooms / apartments),
- low marginal cost during the off-season,
- direct interest in Gazipaşa branding and visibility,
- the ability to make a small, written, non-binding agreement,
- low dependence on bureaucracy.

A municipality contact is valuable but should typically come **after** at least one private anchor partner is interested.

## What we ask for

In a first conversation, we only ask whether the partner is open to:

- a 30–45 day **lean pilot** (or a 60–90 day standard pilot),
- 2–5 partner-supported or discounted rooms / apartments,
- one shared venue session per week,
- one local challenge to prototype around,
- a written, non-binding pilot outline as a next step.

## What we offer in return

- visibility as a co-host of a civic innovation pilot,
- demo-day presence and a public report mention,
- association with international AI / startup founders,
- open documentation of the pilot process,
- workshops for residents and children where appropriate.

## What we do not ask for yet

- no large upfront funding,
- no exclusive contracts,
- no municipal approval as a precondition,
- no transfer of property,
- no long-term commitments,
- no legal, tax, or visa guarantees from the partner.

## Decision rule

The project moves into participant recruitment **only after** at least one anchor partner and at least one local challenge owner are identified and have agreed to a written, non-binding pilot outline. Until then, the project remains in discovery.
