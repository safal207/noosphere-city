# 90-Day Pilot Plan

**Status: proposed / draft.**

This is a working schedule for the first Noosphere City Gazipaşa cohort. Dates are placeholders. The plan exists to make the proposal concrete enough to discuss with partners.

> Before launch, the pilot must select an operator and legal pathway. See [legal_pathways_for_pilot.md](legal_pathways_for_pilot.md) for the preliminary options and the minimum legal checklist.

## Phase 0 — Pre-pilot (weeks -8 to 0)

- Finalize the residency model and challenge tracks.
- Sign at least one housing partner MoU.
- Sign at least one workspace agreement (or confirm a partner-provided room).
- Open applications publicly.
- Secure a non-binding letter of support from the municipality if possible.
- Confirm 3 challenge tracks with named local stakeholders.
- Select 5–10 residents.
- Prepare onboarding materials in English and Turkish.

## Phase 1 — Arrival and onboarding (week 1)

- Housing handover.
- Welcome dinner with local partners.
- Code of conduct and local context briefing.
- Track assignments confirmed.
- Each resident publishes a 1-page plan: problem, hypothesis, milestone at day 30, milestone at day 60, demo at day 90.

## Phase 2 — Build (weeks 2–4)

- Weekly build review (Fridays).
- Field visits: at least one farm, one hotel or marina, one school, one neighborhood walk.
- First public community event: open evening introducing the cohort.
- Day 30 review: each resident presents the first milestone.

## Phase 3 — Pilot with stakeholders (weeks 5–8)

- Residents pair with at least one local stakeholder per track.
- First user tests with real residents, farmers, tourists, or businesses.
- Mid-pilot adjustment: drop or merge tracks that are not working.
- Day 60 review: each resident presents the second milestone.
- One free public workshop, ideally for children or teachers.

## Phase 4 — Ship and demo (weeks 9–12)

- Polish prototypes and documentation.
- Prepare public artifacts: repositories, write-ups, short videos.
- Final dress rehearsal.
- Demo day: open to municipality, partners, residents, and press.
- Public 90-day report published.

## Roles

- **Residency lead.** Owns the program end-to-end.
- **Local liaison.** A Turkish-speaking person who handles relationships with the municipality, partners, and stakeholders.
- **Operations.** Housing, workspace, logistics, payments.
- **Track mentors.** One per active track, ideally with local domain knowledge.
- **Community.** Alumni, volunteers, and visiting practitioners.

## Budget categories (placeholder, not committed)

- Housing (covered by partner or sponsored).
- Workspace (covered by partner or sponsored).
- Operations and local liaison time.
- Demo day venue and catering.
- Modest stipends or travel reimbursements where needed.
- Public report production.

## Risks and mitigations

- **No housing partner found.** Run a remote-only first cohort as a smaller hackathon and use it as proof.
- **Municipality slow to engage.** Proceed with private partners and invite the municipality to demo day.
- **Residents underdeliver.** Tight selection, weekly reviews, and the ability to remove a resident with cause.
- **Local backlash.** Communicate early and clearly; keep workshops free and public.
- **Visa or legal complications.** Keep the pilot short and built on tourist-compatible stays, with formal pathways via technopark partners only when applicable.

## Economic validation

The 90-day pilot should validate:

- whether founders want to join under realistic terms,
- whether housing partners see value,
- whether local businesses sponsor or own real challenges,
- whether prototypes become pilots,
- whether the model can produce at least one paid continuation path,
- whether the cost per useful prototype is acceptable,
- whether the municipality and partners see enough value to continue.

See [economic_model.md](economic_model.md) and [pilot_budget.md](pilot_budget.md).

## Day 90 decision

At the end of the 90 days, the residency lead, the municipality (if engaged), and the housing partner answer one question together: should there be a second cohort, and if so, what changes?
