# Legal Pathways for a Future Pilot

**Status: proposed / draft. Not legal advice.**

This document acknowledges that the legal structure of a real Noosphere City Gazipaşa pilot is unresolved. It outlines preliminary options so partners and reviewers can see that the question is taken seriously, without committing to any specific path.

## 1. Current status

- Noosphere City Gazipaşa is a draft proposal.
- It is not currently a legal entity.
- It has no municipal approval.
- It has no confirmed housing partners.
- It does not currently sponsor visas, residence permits, employment, investment, or work permits.

## 2. Why this matters

A real pilot would involve people, housing, public communication, local partners, and possibly international founders. Before launch, the following must be clarified:

- Legal structure of the pilot operator.
- Status of each participant (visitor, contractor, employee, invitee, none of the above).
- Insurance and liability arrangements.
- Written partner contracts.
- Rules and approvals for public communication.
- Compliance with Turkish law in the relevant areas (housing, tourism, tax, data protection, labor, immigration).

Until these are clarified, the residency exists only as a written proposal.

## 3. Visa and participant status

- Noosphere City does not provide visa, residence, work-permit, tax, employment, or immigration advice.
- Participants remain responsible for their own lawful entry, stay, work authorization, tax status, insurance, and compliance with Turkish law.
- If the pilot involves extended stays or paid work, qualified local legal advice is required before launch.
- No participant should be promised relocation, employment, investment, free housing, or legal status without a formal written agreement.
- Any reference in this repository to Türkiye Tech Visa, technoparks, residence permits, or work permits describes possible future avenues to explore with qualified counsel, not eligibility claims for any individual.

## 4. Possible legal and operator pathways

These options are not mutually exclusive. A real pilot may combine several.

### Option A — Partner-led pilot

A local hotel, apart-hotel, developer, coworking space, association, or business partner acts as the operational host. Noosphere City remains a programme, community, and documentation framework.

- **Pros:** fastest path to a first pilot.
- **Risks:** dependency on a single partner; unclear accountability unless contracts are signed; limited scalability.

### Option B — Technopark or incubator partnership

A Turkish technopark, incubator, or accelerator acts as the institutional partner. This may align with Turkish startup and Tech Visa ecosystem pathways for qualifying founders.

- **Pros:** stronger startup legitimacy; access to existing infrastructure; clearer narrative for international founders.
- **Risks:** slower setup; partner requirements and application processes; potential constraints on programme design.

### Option C — Dedicated Turkish entity later

If traction exists, the project may later explore a Turkish company, association (dernek), foundation (vakıf), or special-purpose pilot entity.

- **Pros:** clearer ownership and operations; ability to enter contracts directly.
- **Risks:** cost, tax, accounting, governance burden; ongoing compliance obligations.

### Option D — Municipality-supported but independently operated

The municipality supports visibility, challenge selection, venue access, and local coordination. The operational pilot is run by a separate responsible partner under one of the options above.

- **Pros:** reduces operational and reputational burden on the municipality.
- **Risks:** public communication must be carefully controlled to avoid implying a municipal programme that does not exist.

## 5. Minimum legal checklist before launch

No participant should be onboarded until the following are in place:

- Written partner agreement with the housing or workspace provider.
- Participant terms (selection, conduct, deliverables, IP, privacy, termination).
- Housing agreement compliant with applicable Turkish rules.
- Insurance and liability review.
- Visa, residence, and work-status review for each participant where relevant.
- Public communication approval process with each named partner.
- Data and privacy review for any data collected during pilots.
- Emergency contact and incident protocol.
- Clear cancellation, withdrawal, and exit policy for both sides.

## 6. Recommended near-term position

- The repository presents Noosphere City as a **proposed pilot only**.
- The most realistic first pilot is **partner-led** (Option A) or **technopark/incubator-supported** (Option B), possibly combined with Option D.
- No final legal pathway is chosen yet.
- The immediate next step is **legal and partner discovery**, not participant recruitment.

Until a legal pathway is selected and the minimum checklist is in place, no public call for residents should be opened.
